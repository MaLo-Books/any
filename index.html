✦   The Mind Behind the Darkness   ✦

MaLo

MaLo writes in the spaces most authors instinctively avoid — the psychological gap between what people do and why they do it, framed by worlds that have no interest in offering comfort.

The work sits at an intersection: silent dread and immediate violence, the cosmic and the deeply personal, the clinical and the visceral. Time, in these stories, is not always linear. Narrators are not always reliable. Dimensions are not always what you assumed.

If you're looking for resolution, closure, or a character you can root for with confidence —
you are in exactly the right place.
